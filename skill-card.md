## Description: <br>
Smart Procurement Navigator assists marketing, business, proposal, bid compilation, and bid decision-making personnel in completing opportunity screening, bid evaluation, procurement document interpretation, evaluation model construction, response plan architecture design, document generation, content polishing, and pre-submission compliance Review. Adapted to the framework of China's Government Procurement Law and Tendering and Bidding Law. <br>

This skill is ready for commercial/non-commercial use. <br>

## Use Case: <br>
Targeted at market development, business management, proposal planning, bid writing, and tendering/bidding decision-making roles, transforming procurement documents into project summaries, evaluation scoring models, response architectures, response document chapter drafts, task boards, and pre-submission Review conclusions. The system assists with the full procurement process, but final bid decisions, Quotation, commitments, signing/sealing, and submission steps remain under manual control. <br>

### Deployment Geography for Use: <br>
China and global Chinese-speaking regions <br>

## Known Risks and Mitigations: <br>
Risk: Procurement documents, Quotation information, client data, qualification documents, personnel files, and historical response documents involve commercially sensitive information. <br>
Mitigation: Deploy only after obtaining organizational authorization; use anonymized or fabricated demonstration files for Demonstration; before formal use, Confirm that storage and retention policies align with compliance requirements. <br>
Risk: System-generated bid recommendations, document chapter drafts, commitment clauses, or risk assessments may be incomplete or misleading when source documents are missing or parsing errors occur. <br>
Mitigation: All outputs must undergo manual review against the original procurement documents, with particular attention to Qualification requirements, evaluation items, rejection clauses, Quotation, delivery commitments, signing/sealing, and final submission. <br>
Risk: Residual reference project files, internal scoring strategy language, exaggerated rhetoric, or templated expressions may remain in formal response documents. <br>
Mitigation: Execute systematic document polishing and consistency Review steps, and verify project names, key data, locations, timelines, and reference text residuals item by item before submission. <br>
